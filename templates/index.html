What the Statuses Mean

Compliant: This dependency's license is on your "allowed" list. No action is needed.

Non-Compliant: This dependency's license is on your "disallowed" list. It likely violates your policy and should be reviewed or replaced.

Review Required: The license was not found in your policy, is complex (e.g., "MIT OR Apache-2.0"), or could not be identified. You must manually review it to ensure it's acceptable for your project.

Common Open-Source License Types

Permissive Licenses

These licenses have minimal restrictions on how you can use, modify, and share the software. They are very business-friendly. You must still include the original copyright and license notice.

Examples: MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD, ISC

Weak Copyleft Licenses

These licenses require you to release your changes to the licensed *library* under the same license. However, they allow you to link to it from your own proprietary software without needing to release your own code.

Examples: LGPL, MPL 2.0, EPL

Strong Copyleft Licenses

These licenses require that any software you build with them must also be released under the same license. This is often called "viral" because the license spreads to the entire derivative work. This is often incompatible with proprietary, closed-source projects.

Examples: GPLv2, GPLv3, AGPLv3
